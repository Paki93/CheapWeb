What is a domain name?

A domain name is the web address people type into their browser to find your website. For example, in www.yourbusiness.co.uk:

Protocol https://

Secure connection between browser and website.

Subdomain www.

Optional part before your main name (www, app, blog).

Name yourbusiness

Your brand or main keyword – this is what people remember.

TLD .co.uk

Top-level domain, such as .co.uk, .com, .org, etc.

Common domain extensions (TLDs)

Different endings (called TLDs) can send different signals to your visitors. Here are some of the most common:

.co.uk

Perfect for UK-based businesses targeting local customers.

  • Trusted in the UK
  • Great for trades, services, shops
  • Feels local and familiar

.com

Global and widely recognised – ideal if you think internationally.

  • Suitable for any kind of business
  • Easy to remember
  • Often first thing people try

.org / .net / others

Good for charities, communities, tech, and specialist projects.

  • .org – non-profits, charities, communities
  • .net – tech, networking, digital services
  • Modern options like .studio, .london, .shop

How to choose a good domain name

You don’t need the “perfect” name – you need a domain that is clear, simple, and trustworthy. Use this checklist:

1. Keep it short

Aim for something that’s easy to type and say out loud. Avoid long sentences, hyphens, or complicated spellings.

2. Make it memorable

Use your brand name, your own name, or a simple phrase that describes what you do (e.g. swiftplumbing.co.uk).

3. Avoid confusion

Avoid numbers and special characters where possible. Ask yourself: “Could I say this once on the phone and they’d spell it correctly?”

4. Think long-term

Choose something that still makes sense if you grow, add services, or expand into new areas.

5. Match your brand

If you already have a business name, try to match it closely. Consistency helps with Google searches and word of mouth.

6. Check social media

It’s a bonus if the same name (or similar) is available on platforms like Instagram or Facebook.

Domain name FAQs

Do I need to buy the domain myself?

No. CheapWeb can register and manage the domain for you. You simply tell us the name you’d like, and we’ll check availability and present options.

Can I use a domain I already own?

Yes. If you already have a domain name, we can connect it to your new website or help you transfer it to our recommended provider.

What happens if my first choice is taken?

We’ll suggest similar alternatives – for example using a different TLD (.co.uk → .com), adding a short word (e.g. “studio”, “uk”), or using your location.

Do I own the domain?

Yes. We register the domain in your name or business name. As long as you keep renewals up to date, the domain is yours.

How long do domains last?

Domains are usually registered yearly (often 1–3 years at a time). We’ll remind you before renewal so you never lose your name.
